About Us

Why Plogging?

We believe in a waste-free environment through social change until it becomes a habit nurturing a healthy generation. We plan to achieve this by recruiting a team in every district in our beloved Jeddah city.
We are a family of friends and colleagues grouped by self-motivation and passion sharing common values and beliefs that treasure the environment, prioritizing it every step of the way. We made running more effective as a promising sport. By adding purpose to our running habit we found out that we are running much more and we are enjoying it. Running is more fun now while collecting trash because we are making sure not to leave any trace behind us and making our environment a better place too.
As a result, within a short period, we recruited 100+ members and the numbers are rapidly growing. It is an amazing sign that we are on the right track!

Note: To cater for different fitness levels across the Jeddah community, some of us jog but the majority walks. We walk and collect on the go and are always encouraging for some jogging to eventually get to the jogging level.
Plogging is a sustainable activity that merges physical activity with caring for the environment. It is sustainable since it is simple, fun, and is linked to well-defined rigid environmental and moral purposes. It also satisfies the 2030 vision to develop eco-friendly practices that can lessen our load on the environment as well as boost the tourism sector.
Tourism can not be built without a decent environment or a habit of keeping it clean.

Inception

It first saw the light as a movement in Sweden, where a group of people started picking up garbage as they were jogging – caring for the environment on the move, and ever since then it has grown phenomenally and has expanded worldwide through USA, Mexico, Scotland and now Saudi Arabia!
Plogging is hence the term given to the act of picking up garbage while jogging - derived from the Swedish term "plocka upp" which means pick up.

The Idea

Because plogging combines running/walking with the environmental dimension in mind, it follows a route. It is not a clean-up activity per say in a sense that we have to clean everything in a defined fixed spot. It is more of a mobile activity that allows us to pickup garbage “on the go” moving from one spot to the other until we get to a particular destination or cover a certain distance. This also means that we can not really clean everything around us because we are always on the move. Plogging is a balance between the environment and physical activity.
Therefore, a plogging session usually starts with defining the route from a particular meeting point for everyone to follow at their own pace. Thanks to Napco and Sanita, the biggest paper packaging company in the Middle East, they are sponsoring us with all the gear that we need. Gear is distributed which includes biodegradable garbage bags, heavy-duty rubber reusable gloves, BPA-free plastic water bottles, trail bags, as well as t-shirts for men and vests for women. At this point, a segregation manual is presented where ploggers are encouraged to split into groups to segregate the waste collected into recyclables like Plastic, Paper, Metal, and Glass. The segregation habit helps not only build a new habit within households but to also facilitate the recycling process of the solid waste collected. The collected segregated waste is then sent to recycling facilities to encourage a circular economy.

It is vital for us to mention that we no longer use the term “waste” to describe our recyclables because of its lost value that we are not even considering or noticing. We strongly believe that plastic for instance can be first reused effectively in many ways before throwing it away in addition to the option of recycling it which sustains our natural resources and embodied energy which could be retrieved into fuel production by the process of pyrolysis. Hence we replaced garbage or waste with the newly refurbished term “THE LOST GOLD”!
To guarantee our sustainability of our plogs, we maintain opting for dynamic crowded areas where most people perform their daily sports and exercises. This helps us send out a clear message of what we are trying to do and make sure the message sinks in raising the awareness we need. We also offer a variety of events. These include habit-introducing mpetitions, in-city plogs (district plogging), remote plogs (outside Jeddah in the form of trips), and collaborative events with existing groups/organisations such as Hejaz Ultra, Blissrunners, JRC, the British international school (BISJ), and the Rawda Community Center.


Our latest activity that was added to our plogging activity involves gathering statistical data through the use of brand audits for mainly plastic products. Brand auditors walk alongside ploggers and quantify both the types and number of plastic items found. These brand audits are then sent to our affiliate worldwide organisation which we are proudly a member of – breakfreefromplastic – to put pressure on international manufacturers of plastic to reinvent eco-friendly alternatives to plastic as means for safe packaging.
